*Customer Service Bulletin — Service Standards & Product Durability Feature*

For a nail service, durability isn't a bonus feature—it is the product. A client pays not just for how their nails look walking out the door, but for how they hold up over the following weeks. When an entire set fails within days, the issue is no longer cosmetic; it is a fundamental breakdown of the craft that leaves the client with nothing to show for their money.

## Gone Before the Second Week

A customer described a rapid and complete failure of her set. "Whole nail came off week one," she reported—the damage beginning almost immediately after the appointment.

It only got worse from there. "Before the second week started all my nails came off," she said, adding that "some nails are missing" altogether. Having paid $50 for the service, she was left with a set that did not survive even a full two weeks. Her verdict was final: "never again will I go there."

## Media Expert's Perspective

A full set losing every nail within two weeks is not a minor durability complaint—it is a total structural failure that points to a breakdown in the fundamentals. Whether the cause was inadequate nail prep, the wrong product, poor adhesion, or a rushed application, the result is the same: the client received essentially zero value for $50. Unlike a chip or a lifted edge that can be touched up, losing the entire set—and even natural nails along with it—signals a competence problem the salon cannot explain away as normal wear. Reviews like this are particularly damaging because they speak to the one thing every prospective client cares about most: will the work last? When the honest answer on display is "not even two weeks," the salon hands future customers a clear reason to book elsewhere.
