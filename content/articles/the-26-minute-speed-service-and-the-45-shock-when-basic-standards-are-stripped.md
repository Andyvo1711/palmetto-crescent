*Customer Service Bulletin — Service Standards & Consistency Feature*

A premium price tag must be accompanied by an appropriate duration and quality of service. When nail salons intentionally cut corners to chase volume, they dismantle the trust of even their most experienced clients. The following complaint is a prime example of a severe lack of service and a breakdown of industry consistency.

## The "Almost Done" Text and the Customer's Disbelief

A customer recounted dropping someone off at the salon at 11:28 am to run some errands. By 11:54 am (a mere 26 minutes later), the customer was absolutely shocked to receive a text message stating, "We are almost done."

"I genuinely thought it was a joke," the outraged customer shared. A $45 "regular" pedicure was completed in under 30 minutes. To make matters worse, this so-called "regular" service didn't even include a basic foot scrub—a step universally considered standard in the nail industry.

With 30 years of experience getting pedicures, the client firmly stated that she had never encountered such a blatant lack of service and corner-cutting.

## Media Expert's Perspective

This beauty establishment is taking the "Stripping to Upsell" strategy to a toxic extreme. Removing an essential step like a foot scrub from a $45 package (which is a premium price for a "regular" service) to force clients into higher-tier packages is a massive pricing failure. Furthermore, completing a pedicure in under 30 minutes proves the technician rushed through the process, turning what should be a relaxing experience into an "express train." A client with 30 years of consumer experience is more than perceptive enough to realize when they are being taken advantage of.
