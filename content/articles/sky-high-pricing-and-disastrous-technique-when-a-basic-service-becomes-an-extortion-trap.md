*Customer Service Bulletin — Pricing Transparency & Competitive Competence Feature*

Today's consumers are highly educated; they are well aware of the local market's pricing standards. When a nail salon arbitrarily prices a basic service far above the average while delivering rock-bottom quality, they don't just lose a client—they expose their lack of transparency and incompetence to the entire online community.

## $55 for a Sloppy "Hack Job"

A customer walked into Holy Nails & Spa with a very minimalist request: just gel French tips on her toes, with absolutely NO pedicure (no soaking, no cuticle cutting). However, at checkout, she was completely shocked to be charged $55 just for the gel polish application.

The core issue is that this pricing was NOT disclosed beforehand. The outraged customer pointed out that other salons around town charge only $35 for the exact same "gel toes with French tip" service.

If the premium price tag came with stellar quality, the customer might have let it slide. Instead, the result was an absolute disaster: "The work was horrendous. Not only did I pay a premium but I got a low quality service and the most uneven job. Paint on my big toe, 3rd toe is a hack job," the furious client described.

## Media Expert's Perspective

This establishment violated two cardinal rules of retail business: "No Upfront Pricing" and "Quality Disconnect." Charging $55 for a service that competitors price at $35 isn't seen as premium positioning; it is viewed by the consumer as outright price gouging. This lack of pricing consistency, coupled with embarrassingly poor technical skills (leaving paint on the skin), provides living proof for the customer to aggressively warn others to stay away from the business.
