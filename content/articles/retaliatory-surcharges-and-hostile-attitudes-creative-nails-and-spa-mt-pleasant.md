*Customer Service Bulletin — Communication Culture & Pricing Transparency Feature*

One of the most crucial skills in the service industry is the ability to accept feedback and perfect a product. However, some technicians view a client's request for adjustments as a personal insult. This latest report exposes the practice of "retaliating" against clients by aggressively tacking on mid-service fees—a move that borders on emotional extortion and completely destroys a business's image.

## The Bad Attitude and the Sudden $15 Charge

A customer requested a design featuring a light pink base with a white line. Noticing that the light pink was missing and the white line was messy, she politely asked the technician to fix the small details and create a thinner, neater line. Instead of gladly correcting her own sloppy work, the technician immediately copped an attitude.

The pinnacle of unprofessionalism occurred right in the middle of the fix. The technician stopped, looked at the client, and coldly announced, "This is going to be an extra 15 dollars just so you know." The customer found this incredibly strange and inappropriate. A request to fix a messy execution should be free, and if there were any legitimate design fees, they should have been disclosed before the service began, not weaponized mid-fix.

## Checkout Ambiguity

The dissatisfaction continued at the register. The total came out to $77. The customer, ready to pay in cash, asked if the price was the same for cash transactions (as many salons waive card processing fees for cash-paying clients). The technician simply said "yes." The customer paid and left, feeling nickel-and-dimed and deeply disrespected by the entire transactional vibe of the establishment.

## Media Expert's Perspective

The technician's behavior in this scenario is a severe violation of professional ethics. Slapping a surcharge on a client in the middle of correcting one's own technical flaw is universally perceived as a "retaliatory fee"—a punishment for daring to speak up. This creates a toxic service environment where clients are afraid to ask for what they want. Furthermore, the lack of friendly transparency regarding payment policies (Cash vs. Card) reinforces the customer's feeling that the salon is simply looking for ways to extort every possible dollar.
