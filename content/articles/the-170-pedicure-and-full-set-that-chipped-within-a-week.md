*Customer Service Bulletin — Pricing & Quality Assurance Feature*

A premium price is a promise: it tells the client to expect not only a pleasant experience, but work that lasts. When a salon leans on upselling and ambiance while the actual craftsmanship fails within days, the price tag becomes the loudest reminder of everything that went wrong. The following complaint against Galaxy Nails Spa is a clear example of how a high bill and poor durability combine to erase a first-time client for good.

## The First Visit, the Upsell, and the $170 Surprise

A customer explained that she came to Galaxy Nails Spa for the very first time, drawn in by its reviews. Once in the chair, "the owner talked me into something completely different than I originally came in asking for—which was fine," she noted. She left with a basic pedicure with gel polish and a full set.

The shock came at checkout. "Never have I ever paid 170 dollars for a pedicure and full set," the client said—an amount far beyond what she had expected to pay for the services she ultimately received.

## Chipping and Lifting Within a Week

The real disappointment, however, was not the price alone but how quickly the work fell apart. "Within a week my nails have began to lift, the top coat on both my nails and my toes has began to chip," she reported.

She was careful to give credit where it was due: "The atmosphere is great." But that was cold comfort. "For 170 there is no reason that this should be happening within a week," she said, adding that she was "very disappointed" and would "likely not return."

## Media Expert's Perspective

Galaxy Nails Spa has fallen into one of the most damaging traps in the beauty industry: charging a luxury price while delivering entry-level durability. Two failures compound here. First, the aggressive upsell—steering a first-time client away from what she asked for and into a $170 combination—sets an expectation of premium quality that the work never met. When a client is guided toward a bigger bill, the burden to deliver flawless, long-lasting results rises with it. Second, gel and a full set lifting and chipping within seven days points to a breakdown in fundamentals: improper prep, poor product, or a rushed application. A great atmosphere can win the first visit, but it cannot survive nails that fail before the next pay period. For $170, the client was entitled to durability—and its absence turned a promising first impression into a lost customer.
